Mechatronics Project:

M/s. Capital Eco Systems has proposed to set up a small scale Common Hazardous waste Incineration Facility for thermal destruction of hazardous waste having characteristics of non-easily biodegradable/toxic/incinerable/combustible in nature. The incinerable waste may be in solid/ semi solid or liquid form. The unit will be set up on an area measuring One Acre in industrially Notified Area at Chamarajanagar in Karnataka developed by Karnataka Industrial Area Development Board. All civic amenities and infrastructure facilities required for industrial developments are available. The industrial area is well connected by railways and by road. As per the Hazardous and Other Wastes (Management and Transboundary Movement) Rules, 2016, hazardous waste generated by industries has to be collected, transported, treated and disposed without causing any environmental damage. The disposal facility should be environmentally sound without causing any adverse impact on health and environment which may result from such waste. M/s. Capital Eco Systems, proposes to collect Incinerable hazardous waste generated from industries for incineration in their proposed facility. Incineration has benefit of reducing volume and toxicity of wastes. The proposed facility will have facility for Incineration designed to meet CPCB standards and will cater to environmentally and economically sound disposal of Hazardous waste generated. The proposed project is well justified in meeting the need of the industries located in the Chamarajanagar, Mysore, Hassan, Mandya etc. The proposed project will have the following facilities: a) KSPCB approved Transport vehicle 2 Nos b) Secured Storage and sorting facility within the plant c) Incinerator of 500 kg/hr capacity with air pollution control equipment d) Effluent treatment and recycling facility e) DG set – 125 KVA

Computer Project:

The project comprises the redevelopment of the existing East Ansari Nagar Campus (106.7 acres) and expansion of the Trauma Center (14.95 acres), measuring a total land parcel of 121.65 acres at East Ansari Nagar campus, New Delhi. The proposed project will include the following: • Increased capacity of the hospital to accommodate an integrated 3000 (new) bedded capacity. • Auditorium and event Center for organization of national and international events. • Residential facilities for in-house doctors, staff and faculty. • Green cover and playground within the campus. • Hostel facilities with equipped facilities such as dining, kitchen, laundry, recreation area, market, etc. for students. • Development of integrated landscape, pedestrian boulevards, and healing gardens. • Multi-level car parking to minimize vehicular traffic on the campus and to develop a smart sustainable campus with vehicles. • An integrated infrastructure including state of the art supports infra including but not limited to AC Plant, DG bank, rooftop solar power plant, etc. This development project comprises of two components (i) demolition and construction to increase FAR in East Ansari Nagar and (ii) construction on vacant plot of Trauma Center Extension. The proposed demolition and new construction on the space will be in phases so that the hospital functions are uninterrupted during redevelopment works. Also, some facilities and buildings will continue to function in their present capacity to support the various functions within the main hospital. The estimated cost of the project is INR 9000 crores.
